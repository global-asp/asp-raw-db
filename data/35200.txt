title:	Ọrẹn ro phuvhẹn  ẹ
author:	Eruotor Grace
artist:	Joe  Werna
lang:	Urhobo
level:	First sentences
lic:	Creative Commons: Attribution 4.0
thumb:	http://africanstorybook.org/illustrations/covers/35200.png
desc:	This story is about the sun as seen through the eyes of a school boy from dawn to dusk.

page_text:

P1	Ọrẹn rọ van cha vwuriọke,@  mi nyo Ogborhuani ro bo
P2	 Ọrẹn van rhe vwẹ egodo rẹ@  Terfa bẹsiẹ ra vwọ riemu@  rurhioke. Mi Nyọrhe rẹ bier vẹ akpupa@  ro brighoro chẹ ovakpo me
P3	Ọrẹn na nyara kpe obuko rurhe@  rẹ evu r’ isukuru ọke rẹ@  emu rogheruvo. Ọrẹn na viri  vwevu@  r’uherevie rẹ otafe.
P4	Ọrẹn na mudia obenu r’uyovwi mẹ. Uhoho mẹ@  ji mudia kerovwẹ. Mẹ v’ Igbeyame heha r’uhoho
P5	Uhoho mẹ rhuaphihọ,ọje havwa phiho. Avware jẹ nene. Uhoho@  mẹ ji gro,@  oje kre. Avware jẹ nene.
P6	Mi mudia, igbeyamẹ mudia. Avware mrẹ uhoho@  ra vware ro gboma. Emudia@  ba vware re, kavware rivwi kpẹ evu ruwevwi@  ẹbe nyono. Avware ko ni@  isukuru kpo.
P7	Ọrẹn na she divwi.@  Me mrẹ ọrẹn na ro shekpo. Me mrẹ uhoho mẹro@  mudia igbẹyi.Oke resherhẹ tere.
P8	Ọrẹn divwiri kpe@  evru re. Mi sherhẹ ukpe mẹ re, me vwerhe evwrhẹn r’ọrẹn r’okpasa oshesheri.
