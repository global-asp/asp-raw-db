desc:	Hares are popular creatures in African folktales. They are famous for behaving badly and always getting away with it. In this Nyungwe folktale

page_text:

P1	Nindu acel,  afoyo obedo kawothu idhu nam.
P2	Rau obedo keca de,  ma ewothu ka camu lum mabecu becu.
P3	Rau nwango oneno ongo nia afoyo obedo keca ka rek egam enyono wii tyend afoyo. Afoyo ugoyo nduru man ecaku kok iwi Rau,  "in i Rau! I copu neno ngo nia itye ka nyonu tyenda?"
P4	Rau ukwayo kisa ibang afoyo,  "Tim ira kisa jarimba. Nwango aneni ongo. Kayo tim ira kisa!" Ento afoyo okwero ni winju man emedire asu ku kok iwi Rau,  "I nyon mi pyem!" Nindu moku,  ibeno! Ibi culu pire!
P5	Kan afoyo ocidhu ka sayu mac man ewacu ire,  "Cidhi,  iwang rau kan ewok kud ipii kacamu lum. Enyonu wii tyenda!" Mac,  ogamu nia,  "Lembe moku ope,  afoyo,  jarimba. Abitimu kite ma ikwayu kude."
P6	Yor ingeye,  rau obedo ka camu lum kama bor ku ndhu nam ma. "Lewu!" Mac umwoc kan ucaku ni wangu yuk wii rau zo.
P7	Rau ucaku ywak man eringo ipii. Yuk wiye zo nwangu odaru wang imac. Rau obedo ka ywak asu,  yuk wiya owang ii mac! I wangu yuk wiya zo! Yuk wiya zo ocidhu! Yuk wiya maleng,  yuk wiya maleng!
P8	Eni re mukethu rau Kendu bicidhu ongo zo kamabor ku dhu pii lworu ma nia mac biwange kude. Afoyo anyongo onego makwe,  ma mac owangu rau,  ewacu,  "Epoy enwanga!"
