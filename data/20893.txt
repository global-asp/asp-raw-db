desc:	Byantaka had a small pot. He desired a bigger pot. What could he do? He used his head and got what he wanted.

page_text:

P1	Byantaka had a cow and a small pot for water.
P2	He went to borrow a bigger pot from his neighbour.
P3	Byantaka put a small pot inside the neighbour’s big pot.
P4	He carried the pots to the neighbour.
P5	“Your pot gave birth,” Byantaka told the neighbour.
P6	Byantaka went to the neighbour to borrow the pot again.
P7	He did not return it. The neighbour went to ask.
P8	Byantaka said, “It died. I was coming to tell you.”
P9	The neighbour shouted, “I have never heard of a pot dying!”
P10	Byantaka replied, “My friend, everything that produces must die.”
P11	The neighbour went to court to report Byantaka.
P12	The judge said, “Every living thing which reproduces must die.”
P13	Byantaka got the big pot and his neighbour lost.
